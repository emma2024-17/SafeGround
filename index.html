What we do

Safety is not a privilege.
It is a right.

SafeGround operates warehouses-turned-sanctuaries across multiple countries. Each one is a temporary home — stocked, staffed, and run by people who have been there.

01

Physical safe houses in multiple countries

Real spaces where young people can stay, eat, sleep, and recover — without fear. Distributed so no single location can be shut down.

02

Medical care and free therapy

On-site medical supplies and therapists who volunteer their time. Partnerships with local hospitals for cases that need more care.

03

Travel assistance and escape support

We help buy tickets for young people who need to physically leave. No one stays trapped because they can't afford a bus ticket.

04

Food and clothing, donated forward

Those who leave donate what they can for those who arrive. A chain of giving built on lived solidarity, not charity.

05

Education and pathways

No young person should leave a safe house with nowhere to go next. We're building education access and job pathways for every resident.

06

Survivor-led, always

This organization is built and run by people with lived experience. We trust those who lived it to lead it.

How it works

From crisis to safety

1

A young person reaches out

Through our contact line or website — anonymously if needed. No forms, no paperwork, no questions that can't wait.

2

We find the nearest safe house

Our network spans multiple countries. We connect them to the closest available location and help arrange travel if needed.

3

They stay, recover, and heal

Food, shelter, medical care, therapy — everything covered. No time limits for those who need longer.

4

They help the next person

When they leave, they donate what they can. Some come back as volunteers, counselors, or staff. The cycle becomes one of healing.
